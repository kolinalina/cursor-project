**Date:** 2026-04-25

Avoid random acts of SEO content.

You need a deliberate strategy. You can either:

- Set a specific quantity to publish per week.
- Treat your SEO content like a sprint: focus on one cluster for two weeks, build out all content in that cluster, then launch it.

You need to be strategic. Random publishing is rarely going to accomplish much.

## Post 4
**Date:** 2026-04-20

Most advice about "AI SEO" is overcomplicated.

At the end of the day, the best way to get your brand visible in AI answers is still to do strong SEO and focus on search-everywhere optimization.

If your brand has strong visibility across search surfaces and core retrieval sources, you are far more likely to show up in AI answers.

SEO is the lead domino.

That does not only mean SEO on your website, even though that is where you should start.

- You should also think about SEO on YouTube.
- You should think about visibility across every important retrieval source.

The number one retrieval source is still search.

Your brand needs to be present on the sources these systems are actually pulling from.

I use Rankability to quickly find retrieval source gaps for any topic. These become outreach targets.

That gives you a clear path: increase visibility on retrieval sources that already influence AI answers.

Do that consistently, and you give yourself a much better chance of showing up more often.

There is no real secret:

- Consistent technical SEO on your website.
- Building topic authority on your website.
- Extending that authority across your other owned assets.
- Earning off-site visibility on sources AI systems trust and retrieve from.

That is how you win.
