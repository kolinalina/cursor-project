My agency, The Search Initiative, recently helped an online music education site grow organic sessions by 26.57% in 6 months (from 3,974 to 5,030 monthly sessions).

- Review high-visibility blog content and improve clarity, depth, and usefulness
- Align content with search intent, not only target keywords
- Refine headings, formatting, and flow for easy scanning
- Strengthen links between informational content and core offerings

### 2) Site Structure and Internal Linking
- Optimize page titles for clarity and click-through potential
- Improve internal linking between related pages
- Simplify navigation menus
- Surface high-performing content more prominently
- Organize content into clear topic pathways

### 3) Brand Page Optimization
- Ensure your About page clearly communicates your background and mission
- Explain why your business exists in a way that feels human
- Articulate your value proposition clearly

Use storytelling and real-world context over corporate speak.

### 4) E-E-A-T and Trust Optimization
- Add clear business information in the footer (name, address, contact details)
- Create a dedicated author page with experience, credentials, and expertise
- Add author bios across all blog content
- Remove ambiguity about who you are, what you do, and why you are qualified

These are no longer optional. Google expects to see them.

## Post 4
**Date:** 2026-03-26

Google Search Console just got a major upgrade, and most SEOs are ignoring an urgent data problem hiding inside it.

Here are 5 things you should do in GSC right now:

### 1) Export historical data immediately
GSC keeps only 16 months of data.

That means your pre-AI Overviews baseline from late 2023 and early 2024 is disappearing now. Without it, you cannot measure the real impact AI Overviews had on traffic.

- Go to Performance > Search Results
- Set the date range as far back as possible
- Export everything to a spreadsheet

Do it now. Once it is gone, it is gone.

### 2) Set up the new branded vs. non-branded filter
Google rolled this out to eligible sites.

It separates branded queries (people searching your name) from non-branded queries (people discovering you).

- Branded up, non-branded flat -> content strategy needs work
- Non-branded up, branded flat -> traffic is growing, but brand is not
- Both up -> strong signal

Check Google’s auto-classification; some brands are misclassified.

### 3) Use the new AI-powered query feature
You can use natural-language prompts directly in GSC.

Example:

"Show me mobile queries with high impressions but low CTR in the last 28 days."

Use this to find quick wins. Page-1 rankings with low CTR are often your biggest opportunities.

### 4) Track AI Overview exposure
33% of AI Overview links come from top-10 results.

But 47% come from pages ranking 50th or lower.

That means pages you considered weak might still be cited by AI.

- Check which pages appear in AI Overviews (Ahrefs or Semrush)
- Cross-reference with GSC to see whether citations drive impressions
- Prioritize updates with fresh stats and better structure

### 5) Build a quarterly audit routine
Google's algorithm changes 12+ times per day.

Set reminders for Jan 1, Apr 1, Jul 1, and Oct 1.

On audit day:

- Pull a GSC comparison report (current vs. 3 months ago)
- Flag keywords that dropped off page 1
- Check whether search intent shifted
- Re-optimize dropped pages with fresh data and updated structure

SEOs who treat GSC as a daily tool will outperform those checking it once a month.

## Post 5
**Date:** 2026-03-26

AI Overviews now appear on 20%+ of all US keywords.

If you rank #1 on a SERP with an AI Overview, clicks drop by 34.5% on average.

You are not always losing rankings. You are often losing clicks to Google's own AI answers.

How to get AI to cite your content instead of competitors:

### 1) Write direct, extractable answers
AI does not read every word of your page. It pulls passages.

Make paragraphs stand on their own. Place a bold, direct answer beneath each heading and avoid fluff.

### 2) Increase fact density
AI systems prioritize unique data points.

- Add original stats, case studies, and specific numbers
- Include comparison tables
- Build FAQ sections for "People Also Ask" questions
- Add definition boxes and step-by-step lists

Generic content gets ignored. Fact-dense content gets cited.

### 3) Use schema markup across the site
Structured data helps AI crawlers understand content faster.

- Article schema for blog posts
- FAQPage schema for Q&A content
- HowTo schema for tutorials
- Organization schema for About pages
- Product schema for e-commerce pages

Also check `robots.txt` and ensure relevant AI crawlers are not blocked.

### 4) Keep content fresh
Recency matters in source selection.

A stale guide can lose to a newer article, even if your original version is better.

- Add a visible "Last updated" timestamp
- Refresh stats and examples quarterly
- Update cornerstone content first

### 5) Measure what matters
Traditional SEO metrics alone are no longer enough.

Track:

- AI citation frequency
- Share of voice across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity
- Citation sentiment
- AI-referred traffic (GA4 attribution)

Tools like Ahrefs Brand Radar can help monitor prompt-level visibility at scale.
