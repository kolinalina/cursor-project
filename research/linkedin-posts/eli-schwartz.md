## Post 4
**Date:** 2026-04-23

Some SEO teams genuinely thought they had cracked the code:

AI-generated content at scale, thousands of pages, and programmatic everything.

It was called strategy, but it was closer to machine translation with a keyword list.

Glenn Gabe calls this "Mt AI," and the term fits.

Google updates did not just slow these teams down-they wiped out years of work in a few algorithm cycles. Traffic disappeared, and smug case studies were quietly deleted.

The question many ask is: "How do we recover?"

Wrong question.

You cannot recover by doing more of the same thing that triggered the problem. Publishing "better" AI content is not a pivot; it is slower self-destruction.

The real pivot:

- Stop asking what keywords you can rank for.
- Start asking what problems users actually have.
- Build content around intent, not search volume.
- Write things people would share even if Google did not exist.

Do not try to outsmart the algorithm. Focus on being genuinely useful to real people, and traffic follows.

Nobody wanted to hear this when content farms were printing traffic.

Now they are finally hearing it.
