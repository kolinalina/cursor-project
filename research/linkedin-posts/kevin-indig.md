## Post 2
**Date:** 2026-04-24

You might have noticed that AI models do not treat every type of website the same way when putting together answers.

Recognized consumer brands get mentioned in output quite frequently. AI often names them outright without always needing to explicitly link them as a source.

A different pattern appears for publishers and content aggregators. They are often cited as reference material but rarely mentioned by name. For example, Medium was cited 16 times in the Semrush data I recently analyzed, but not named once.

For businesses whose core value depends on being recognized as an information authority, this creates an important dynamic to navigate.

## Post 4
**Date:** 2026-04-22

It looks like there is no universal rule for how AI engines handle visibility. Semrush data shows they often treat citations and mentions very differently.

Gemini tends to name brands in over 83% of its appearances, but only generates a citation link around 21% of the time. It behaves a bit like a conversationalist drawing on general brand knowledge.

ChatGPT leans the other way. It cites sources about 87% of the time, but only mentions brands in 20% of answers, acting more like a research paper with footnotes.

For marketing teams, this suggests that visibility on one platform does not guarantee the same result on another. Tailoring your approach to how specific engines operate may be a smart next step.

## Post 5
**Date:** 2026-04-21

We often assume that getting a link in an AI overview means people are seeing our brand. That might not always be true.

AI models often use content but do not explicitly name the creator. I analyzed Semrush data and found that around 62% of brand citations fly under the radar. The domain gets a source link, but the brand name is left out of the answer text.

Only about 13% of the appearances I looked at converted into both a citation and a mention.

Being cited generally means AI is drawing on your content, while being mentioned means it is naming you. These are two distinct outcomes.

Optimizing for a link does not automatically get your name in front of users, so it is worth considering both angles when planning content.
