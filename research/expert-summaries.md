## 1. Aleyda Solís

**Key Themes:**
- AI search measurement and attribution — how to prove AI influence on business outcomes
- The shift from traditional SEO metrics (rankings, clicks, sessions) to AI visibility metrics
- Google's AI Mode expansion and what it means for content strategy
- Digital PR and authority building in the AI search era
- Agentic commerce (ChatGPT shopping, OpenAI instant checkout) and its SEO implications

**Top Recommendations:**
- Use a 3-Layer Framework to measure AI search success: Presence (are you visible?), Readiness (are your structural conditions right?), Business Impact (is visibility translating to revenue?)
- Add a post-signup survey question: "Did you come across our brand in an AI assistant before signing up?" — one of the strongest first-party proxies for AI-driven influence
- Don't just track traffic — track brand mentions and citations across AI platforms separately
- Build digital PR for authority, not just backlinks — citations in AI answers require trusted third-party mentions
- Don't block Google-Extended bot — it doesn't help and fanouts can still use your indexed content

**Quotes / Strong Statements:**
- "None of these metrics prove AI influence on their own. The value comes from reading them as a set." (LinkedIn Post 1)
- "Presence tells you where the brand appears. Readiness tells you why it looks that way. Business Impact tells you whether that visibility creates measurable value." (LinkedIn Post 2)
- "AI has lowered the barrier for content creation, forcing Google to raise the bar for what actually gets indexed." (LinkedIn Post 3)

**Unique Angle:**
Aleyda is the most measurement-focused expert on the list. While others talk about tactics, she builds frameworks for proving ROI from AI SEO. Her 3-layer framework is the most structured approach to AI search measurement found in this research.

## 2. Nathan Gotch

**Key Themes:**
- "Search Everywhere Optimization" — SEO is no longer just about Google
- AI referral traffic is real and trackable (ChatGPT leads among AI sources)
- Daily SEO habits vs. random publishing — systems beat hustle
- Topical clustering and content strategy as the foundation of AI visibility
- Trend-based content as a competitive advantage

**Top Recommendations:**
- Optimize existing content first — use Google Search Console to find keywords ranking positions 2-15 (low-hanging fruit)
- Build "Search Everywhere Optimization" — be present on YouTube, Reddit, Quora, and other retrieval sources that AI systems pull from
- Avoid "random acts of SEO content" — either publish on a set schedule or batch-publish full topic clusters at once
- Use trending topics (TikTok, Reddit, Exploding Topics) to find emerging keywords before SEO tools catch up
- Strong SEO is still the foundation — AI visibility follows from traditional search visibility

**Quotes / Strong Statements:**
- "Google: 10,302 sessions. ChatGPT: 348. The future is not Google OR AI. It is Google + AI." (LinkedIn Post 3)
- "Most advice about AI SEO is overcomplicated. The best way to get visible in AI answers is to do strong SEO and focus on search-everywhere optimization." (LinkedIn Post 4)
- "SEO is the lead domino." (LinkedIn Post 4)

**Unique Angle:**
Nathan is the most systems-oriented expert. His core message is that AI SEO isn't a new discipline — it's an extension of good SEO discipline. He's also one of the few to publish actual traffic numbers showing the current Google vs. AI search split.

## 3. Michael King

**Key Themes:**
- AI search as an "inclusion vs. exclusion" problem, not a rankings problem
- Training data as the hidden factor deciding winners in AI search
- Content must be verifiable by AI systems, not just well-written
- "Answer Gap" — the gap between brands that AI can verify and those it can't
- Sentiment in AI answers may matter more than position

**Top Recommendations:**
- Focus on "answer readiness" — can AI systems actually verify what you're claiming?
- Use topic modeling to build content that AI systems recognize as authoritative
- AI search sentiment (how AI answers describe your brand) matters as much as citation frequency
- Content differentiation is now the core SEO problem — generic content gets excluded from AI answers entirely
- Your content has a "mathematical footprint" — AI systems score content structure, not just keywords

**Quotes / Strong Statements:**
- "The brands showing up in AI answers are not winning because they have better content. They are winning because AI systems can actually verify what they are saying." (LinkedIn Post 4)
- "We are not just talking about ranking anymore. We are talking about inclusion versus exclusion." (LinkedIn Post 4)
- "Training data is quietly deciding winners in AI search." (YouTube transcript title)

**Unique Angle:**
Michael King is the most technically rigorous expert on the list. He approaches AI SEO from a data science and machine learning perspective. His concept of "answer readiness" and content verifiability goes deeper than any other expert's framework.

## 4. Ryan Doser

**Key Themes:**
- AI search is overrated for small businesses — focus on ROI-positive channels first
- LLMs are easier to manipulate than Google ever was
- Trust signals for LLMs: relevant backlinks, podcast appearances, published authorship
- AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) as the emerging discipline replacing traditional SEO
- Claude Code as a legitimate SEO workflow tool

**Top Recommendations:**
- For small/medium businesses: prioritize paid traffic, email, and social before AI search optimization
- For large brands: AI search citation management is critical for narrative control
- Build trust signals LLMs reward: high-relevance backlinks (not volume), podcast guest appearances, published books/articles
- Use Claude Code for building SEO workflows and landing pages — not just for content generation
- Subject matter expertise matters more now than ever — AI content without real expertise gets filtered out

**Quotes / Strong Statements:**
- "If you're a small to medium size business, AI search is very overrated and shouldn't be prioritized." (YouTube: AI Search is a Shiny Object)
- "LLMs are actually easier to manipulate than traditional Google search results." (YouTube: LLMs Are Easier to Manipulate)
- "The bottleneck is not the model anymore. It is you." (LinkedIn Post 2)

**Unique Angle:**
Ryan Doser is the most contrarian voice on AI search hype. His "shiny object" framing for AI search is a useful counterbalance to the panic-driven content from other experts. He's also the most explicit about LLM manipulation vulnerabilities, which has playbook implications.

## 5. Kevin Indig

**Key Themes:**
- Different AI platforms (Gemini, ChatGPT) treat citations and brand mentions very differently
- AI Overviews reduce organic clicks significantly — data-backed analysis
- Getting featured in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini requires different strategies per platform
- Change management is underrated — teams with better processes get more out of AI SEO
- Hyper-targeted content matters more now than it did under Google-only SEO

**Top Recommendations:**
- Tailor content strategy per AI platform — Gemini names brands but rarely links, ChatGPT links but rarely names brands
- Track both citations AND brand mentions separately — they're different outcomes with different values
- Avoid JavaScript-heavy sites — most LLM crawlers don't render JavaScript
- Check robots.txt and CDN settings — many sites accidentally block LLM crawlers
- Produce hyper-targeted content for specific audience segments, not broad keyword clusters

**Quotes / Strong Statements:**
- "Gemini names brands in 83% of appearances but only generates a citation link 21% of the time. ChatGPT cites sources 87% of the time but only mentions brands in 20% of answers." (LinkedIn Post 4)
- "62% of brand citations fly under the radar — the domain gets a source link but the brand name is left out." (LinkedIn Post 5)
- "We've gotten very lazy about [understanding our audience] because performance marketing has been so good." (YouTube: 3 blockers to ranking in AI search)

**Unique Angle:**
Kevin Indig is the most data-driven expert on platform-specific AI behavior. His Semrush data analysis on how Gemini vs. ChatGPT handle citations is uniquely valuable and not covered by any other expert in this research.

## 6. Matt Diggity

**Key Themes:**
- AI can run entire SEO campaigns — tested and proven on real client sites
- Reddit is the highest-trust source for AI search answers right now
- AI Overviews appear on 20%+ of US keywords — clicks drop 34.5% when they appear
- Building custom ChatGPT agents for content auditing
- E-E-A-T and trust signals are now mandatory, not optional

**Top Recommendations:**
- Build Reddit presence strategically — AI tools (ChatGPT, Gemini, AI Overviews) cite Reddit more than almost any other source
- Write direct, extractable answers — AI pulls passages, not whole pages. Bold answers under every heading
- Increase fact density — original stats, case studies, specific numbers get cited over generic content
- Use schema markup across all content types (Article, FAQPage, HowTo, Organization)
- Export GSC historical data now — 16-month limit means pre-AI-Overview baseline is disappearing

**Quotes / Strong Statements:**
- "I built an AI bot that increased my client's revenue from $166K to $491K per month by increasing monthly traffic by 266%." (YouTube: I Let an AI Run my SEO Campaign)
- "AI Overviews now appear on 20%+ of all US keywords. If you rank #1 on a SERP with an AI Overview, clicks drop by 34.5% on average." (LinkedIn Post 5)
- "Generic content gets ignored. Fact-dense content gets cited." (LinkedIn Post 5)

**Unique Angle:**
Matt Diggity is the most results-oriented expert — he tests everything on real money sites and publishes the numbers. His Reddit strategy for AI search visibility is the most specific, actionable tactic found in this entire research.

## 7. Eli Schwartz

**Key Themes:**
- Brand marketing — not AEO checklists — is the real path to LLM visibility
- AI-generated content at scale is self-destructive ("Mt AI" phenomenon)
- SEO roadmaps are task lists dressed up as strategy — hypothesis-driven SEO is the alternative
- Stop optimizing for keywords, start optimizing for intent and real user problems
- Strong existing SEO = strong LLM visibility, with little additional effort needed

**Top Recommendations:**
- Invest in brand marketing as the primary LLM visibility strategy — not AEO checklists or Reddit astroturfing
- Develop hypotheses before starting SEO work — "do this because X will happen" not just a task list
- Build content people would share even if Google didn't exist
- Stop asking what keywords you can rank for — start asking what problems users actually have
- Don't try to recover from AI content penalties by publishing "better" AI content — that's slower self-destruction

**Quotes / Strong Statements:**
- "The best path to improve LLM visibility is brand marketing — not AEO checklists sold by agencies, AI-generated content, or Reddit astroturfing citation campaigns." (LinkedIn Post 3)
- "Publishing 'better' AI content is not a pivot; it is slower self-destruction." (LinkedIn Post 4)
- "If a company already has strong search visibility from brand strength and effective SEO, it will likely have high LLM visibility with little additional effort." (LinkedIn Post 3)

**Unique Angle:**
Eli Schwartz is the most skeptical voice on AI SEO tactics. His argument that brand marketing beats AEO checklists is a direct counter to almost every other expert on this list. He's the essential "devil's advocate" perspective that prevents the playbook from becoming a tactics checklist.

Nicolas Gorroño is the most ROI-focused expert on AI traffic quality. His 15.9% conversion rate data point for AI-referred traffic vs. 1.7-2.8% for organic is one of the most compelling statistics in this entire research — and a strong argument for prioritizing AI search visibility.

## 10. Rand Fishkin

**Key Themes:**
- Zero-click era is real — web strategy must shift from traffic to awareness and intent
- AI visibility relies heavily on "best of" lists and third-party mentions
- Don't replace real data with AI prompt outputs — AI confidently gives wrong data
- Getting into AI results is fundamentally different from ranking in Google
- Brand-first strategy is the only sustainable path in AI search

**Top Recommendations:**
- Get your brand on "best of" lists — brands featured on more lists (and higher up) have outsized AI visibility
- Never use AI prompts as a replacement for real keyword/audience data — AI gives confident but incorrect data
- Shift web strategy from traffic generation to brand awareness and intent creation
- "Search Everywhere Optimization" — be present across all platforms your audience uses
- Understand that AI search inclusion requires a different strategy from Google ranking

**Quotes / Strong Statements:**
- "Not every business can survive the zero-click era. If your web strategy is centered on driving traffic rather than earning awareness, trust, and intent to act, you are in serious trouble." (LinkedIn Post 2)
- "If you copy and paste [an AI prompt for keyword data] over to ChatGPT, it will confidently give you data that is not correct." (YouTube: Don't Fall For AI Prompt Replacements)
- "Getting into ChatGPT or Google's AI mode is fundamentally different from getting into the top of Google search results." (YouTube: Mike King on SparkToro)

**Unique Angle:**
Rand Fishkin is the most strategic, big-picture thinker on the list. His zero-click era framing and "best of lists" insight about AI visibility are perspectives not found in any other expert's content. He also provides the strongest warning against over-relying on AI for data research.

## Cross-Expert Pattern Summary

**What most experts agree on:**
1. Strong traditional SEO is still the foundation of AI visibility
2. Brand presence and third-party mentions are critical for LLM citations
3. Generic content is getting filtered out — fact-dense, specific content wins
4. Schema markup and technical SEO basics are now more important than ever
5. Measurement needs to evolve — track citations + mentions + AI-referred traffic separately

**Where experts clearly disagree:**
1. Volume vs. quality: Julian Goldie (publish daily with AI) vs. Eli Schwartz (brand marketing beats content volume)
2. AI search priority for SMBs: Ryan Doser (it's a shiny object, deprioritize) vs. Nicolas Gorroño (AI traffic converts 5x better, prioritize it)
3. Tactics vs. strategy: Matt Diggity (specific Reddit tactics, agent building) vs. Rand Fishkin (tactics won't save you without strategic shift)
4. AEO checklists: Most experts provide them vs. Eli Schwartz (AEO checklists are sold by agencies and miss the point)
